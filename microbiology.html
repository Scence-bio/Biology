🦠 Microbiology - Study of Microorganisms

Study of microscopic organisms and their interactions

Microbiology is the scientific study of microorganisms, including bacteria, viruses, fungi, protozoa, and other microscopic life forms. This field examines the structure, function, classification, and interactions of these tiny organisms with each other and their environment. Microbiology is fundamental to understanding infectious diseases, developing antibiotics and vaccines, food preservation, biotechnology, and environmental processes. The field includes medical microbiology (study of pathogenic microbes), environmental microbiology (microbes in ecosystems), industrial microbiology (use of microbes in manufacturing), and molecular microbiology (genetic and biochemical aspects of microbes).
