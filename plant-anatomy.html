Dicot Root Cross Section
A dicot root is the underground part of a dicot plant, typically with xylem arranged in a star shape and phloem between the arms. It functions mainly in anchorage, water and mineral absorption, and food storage.
Structures:
Epidermis - Outer single layer absorbing water and minerals
Cortex - Storage and water movement to vascular tissue
Endodermis - Regulates entry into vascular tissue
Pericycle - Initiates lateral root growth
Xylem - Star-shaped arrangement for water transport
Phloem - Between xylem arms for nutrient transport

  • Anchorage and support
  • Water and mineral absorption
  • Food storage
  • Transport of materials

Monocot Root Cross Section
A monocot root is characterized by a ring of vascular bundles surrounding a large central pith. It mainly functions in anchorage, water and mineral absorption, and food storage.
Structures:

Cortex - Food storage and water transport
Endodermis - Regulates water and mineral movement
Pericycle - Gives rise to lateral roots
Xylem - Ring arrangement for water conduction
Phloem - Between xylem vessels
Pith - Large central nutrient storage region
‚öôÔ∏è Functions:
  • Anchorage in soil

Lower Epidermis - Contains stomata for gas exchange

Cortex - Support and food storage (collenchyma and parenchyma)
Endodermis - Regulates flow into vascular bundles
Vascular Bundles - Arranged in ring with xylem and phloem
Cambium - Meristematic tissue for secondary growth
Pith (Medulla) - Central nutrient storage
‚öôÔ∏è Functions:

Tissue Type - Secondary xylem (wood tissue)

Raphides (Calcium Oxalate Crystals)
Raphides are needle-shaped crystals of calcium oxalate found in plant cells. They act as a defense mechanism against herbivores due to their irritating nature.
Features:
Material Composition - Calcium oxalate crystals
Shape - Thin, elongated, and pointed (needle-like)
Arrangement - Often clustered or scattered within cells
Location - Found in leaves, stems, or tubers
‚öôÔ∏è Functions:
  • Plant defense against herbivores
  • Causes irritation when ingested
  • Calcium regulation in plant tissues
  • Deterrent to insects and animals

Features:
Cell Characteristics - Living, elongated, unevenly thickened corners
Wall Composition - Cellulose and pectin-rich primary walls
Appearance - Compactly arranged, thickened at corners
Location - Below epidermis in young stems and leaf veins
‚öôÔ∏è Functions:
  • Mechanical support for growing parts
  • Flexibility during plant movement
  • Structural integrity in young organs
  • Support without restricting growth
