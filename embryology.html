Components:
Blastomeres
Yolk
Cleavage furrows
Animal pole
Vegetal pole
📍 Location:
Dividing zygote of frog embryo: vegetal pole of egg, between blastomeres, upper region of egg, lower region of egg
⚙️ Functions:
  • Form the cells of the early embryo
  • Provide nutrition for development
  • Help separate dividing cells
  • Site of rapid cell division (animal pole)
  • Site of slower cell division and nutrient storage (vegetal pole)

Slide 9
Frog Neurula Stage
Components:
Neural tube
Notochord
Somites
Tail bud
Archenteron
Yolk cells
📍 Location:
Dorsal midline of embryo, below neural tube, lateral sides of notochord, posterior end of embryo, ventral cavity, lower part of embryo
⚙️ Functions:
  • Develops into the central nervous system
  • Provides axial support and signals for development
  • Forms muscles and vertebrae
  • Initiates tail formation
  • Primitive gut cavity
  • Provides nutrition to the embryo
Slide 10
Frog Zygote (Fertilized Egg)
Components:
Blastomeres
Animal pole
Vegetal pole
Cleavage furrow
Gray crescent
📍 Location:
Entire zygote, upper hemisphere of egg, lower hemisphere of egg, across the egg, opposite sperm entry point
⚙️ Functions:
  • Form embryonic cells
  • Undergo rapid division, form micromeres
  • Undergo slower division, form macromeres
  • Divide the zygote during cleavage
  • Establish body axis and initiate gastrulation
Slide 11
Four Cell Stage (Second Cleavage)
Components:
Blastomeres
Cleavage furrow (second)
Animal pole
Vegetal pole
Vitelline membrane
📍 Location:
Entire egg (four equal or unequal cells), vertical plane perpendicular to first furrow, upper region of egg, lower region of egg, surrounding the egg cell
⚙️ Functions:
  • Form early embryonic structure
  • Divide the embryo into four blastomeres
  • Site of faster cell division
  • Site of slower cell division due to yolk
  • Protects the egg and maintains shape
Slide 12
Neurulation Stage (Neural Tube Formation)
Components:
Neural folds
Neural groove
Neural tube
Notochord
Somites
Archenteron
Yolk cells
Ectoderm
Mesoderm
Endoderm
📍 Location:
Sides of dorsal midline, between neural folds, mid dorsal region (after folds fuse), below neural tube, lateral to notochord, ventral cavity, lower part of embryo, outer layer, middle germ layer, inner germ layer
⚙️ Functions:
  • Elevate and form neural tube
  • Initial depression forming neural tube
  • Forms brain and spinal cord
  • Provides support and signals to surrounding tissues
  • Give rise to muscle and vertebrae
  • Forms the primitive gut
  • Store and supply nutrients
  • Forms skin and nervous tissue
  • Forms muscles, bones, and circulatory system
  • Forms digestive and respiratory linings
Slide 13
Gastrulation Stage
Components:
Blastopore
Dorsal lip of blastopore
Archenteron
Ectoderm
Endoderm
Yolk plug
Mesoderm
📍 Location:
Posterior region of embryo, upper margin of blastopore, internal cavity forming behind blastopore, outer surface of embryo, inner lining of archenteron, center of blastopore opening, between ectoderm and endoderm
⚙️ Functions:
  • Opening through which cells migrate inward
  • Initiates gastrulation and mesoderm formation
  • Forms the primitive gut
  • Forms skin and nervous system
  • Forms digestive and respiratory tract
  • Temporary plug of yolky cells during invagination
  • Forms muscles, bones, and circulatory tissues
Slide 14
Blastula Stage
Components:
Blastocoel
Blastomeres
Animal pole
Vegetal pole
Yolk
📍 Location:
Central cavity of blastula, surrounding blastocoel, upper hemisphere of blastula, lower hemisphere of blastula, inside vegetal blastomeres
⚙️ Functions:
  • Space for cell migration during gastrulation
  • Develop into embryonic tissues
  • Forms ectoderm
  • Forms endoderm and stores yolk
  • Provides nutrients to embryo
Slide 15
Mid-Gastrula Stage
Components:
Blastopore
Yolk plug
Archenteron
Ectoderm
Endoderm
Mesoderm
📍 Location:
Opening at posterior side, center of blastopore, internal cavity above yolk plug, outer layer, inner layer surrounding archenteron, between ectoderm and endoderm
⚙️ Functions:
  • Entry point for gastrulation cell movement
  • Remaining yolk-filled cells being enclosed
  • Future digestive tract
  • Forms skin and nervous system
  • Forms internal organs like gut lining
  • Forms muscles, notochord, and circulatory system
Slide 16
Morula Stage (16-Cell Stage)
Components:
Micromeres
Macromeres
Cleavage furrows
Blastomeres
📍 Location:
Upper (animal pole) region, lower (vegetal pole) region, between dividing blastomeres, entire morula mass
⚙️ Functions:
  • Divide rapidly; contribute to ectoderm
  • Store yolk; contribute to endoderm
  • Indicate division planes
  • Form all embryonic tissues
Slide 17
Early Blastula Stage
Components:
Blastocoel
Micromeres
Macromeres
Blastoderm
Blastomeres
📍 Location:
Central fluid-filled cavity, animal pole (upper region), vegetal pole (lower region), layer of cells surrounding blastocoel, entire cellular mass
⚙️ Functions:
  • Space for cell movement during gastrulation
  • Form ectoderm
  • Form endoderm and store yolk
  • Develops into embryonic germ layers
  • Give rise to all body tissues
Slide 18
Eight Cell Stage (Third Cleavage)
Components:
Blastomeres
Micromeres
Macromeres
📍 Location:
Entire cellular mass, animal pole (upper region), vegetal pole (lower region)
⚙️ Functions:
  • Give rise to all body tissues
  • Form ectoderm
  • Form endoderm and store yolk
Slide 19
Neural Plate Stage
Components:
Neural plate
Notochord
Neural folds
Archenteron
Somites
📍 Location:
Dorsal midline (surface ectoderm), just beneath neural plate (mesoderm), lateral edges of neural plate, ventral to notochord (primitive gut cavity), on either side of notochord
⚙️ Functions:
  • Forms central nervous system (brain and spinal cord)
  • Induces neural plate formation, forms axial support
  • Elevate and fuse to form neural tube
  • Develops into digestive tract
  • Form muscles, vertebrae, and dermis
Slide 20
Tadpole Stage (Larval Form)
Components:
Head
Eyes
External gills (in early tadpole)
Tail
Notochord
Somites
Brain region
Heart (may be visible)
Intestine
📍 Location:
Anterior region, on either side of head, lateral to head (early stages), posterior elongated part, along midline of body axis, segmentally arranged on both sides of notochord, front dorsal side of head, ventral side behind head, coiled in mid-body region
⚙️ Functions:
  • Contains brain and sensory organs
  • Vision
  • Temporary respiration in water
  • Swimming and locomotion
  • Structural support
  • Develop into muscles and vertebrae
  • Controls nervous activity
  • Circulates blood
  • Digestion and absorption
